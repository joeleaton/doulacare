About Doulas

A doula provides continuous physical, emotional, and informational support to you and your partner before, during, and after birth. I help with:

  • Physical comfort: Massage, positioning, breathing techniques, use of birth ball, rebozo, TENS machine, etc.
  • Emotional support: Encouragement, reassurance, a calm presence, and helping you feel safe and confident
  • Information: Explaining what's happening, discussing your options, helping you make informed decisions
  • Partner support: Showing your partner how they can help, allowing them breaks, reducing their stress
  • Advocacy: Ensuring your wishes are heard and respected

Unlike midwives who provide medical care, doulas focus entirely on your wellbeing and experience. We don't perform medical tasks - we're there for YOU.

Midwives provide medical care. They:

  • Monitor your baby's heartbeat
  • Check your blood pressure and vitals
  • Perform vaginal examinations
  • Deliver your baby
  • Handle medical interventions if needed
  • Care for multiple women during their shift

Doulas provide non-medical support. We:

  • Stay with YOU continuously throughout labour
  • Focus on your comfort and emotional needs
  • Never leave your side for shift changes
  • Provide techniques for coping with contractions
  • Support your birth partner
  • Help you understand your options

We work together as a team! You need a midwife for medical care, and a doula for continuous personal support. Think of it this way: the midwife cares for your body and baby, while the doula cares for your experience and emotions.
